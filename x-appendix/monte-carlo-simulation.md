## Why Monte Carlo?

A deterministic model shows you the ideal case. Reality is not ideal. Nodes get sick, clients cancel projects, markets shift. A Monte Carlo simulation runs the same scenario **100 times** with randomized productivity variance (±20% per node per month) and averaged idle rates. The numbers you see below are not best-case fantasies — they are the **averaged result** of 100 independent runs of a stochastic engine. This is the same methodology used by financial institutions, aerospace engineers, and nuclear physicists to model complex systems under uncertainty.

The variance collapses predictably at scale: a 7-node Pod has high individual variance (one sick Elder changes everything), but a 2,070-node Subgrid has a variance approaching zero by the Central Limit Theorem. This is itself a proof of antifragility — the larger the organism, the more statistically predictable its output.

## The Idle Rate Model

The "idle rate" simulates real-world market conditions:

| Idle Rate | Meaning | Real-World Analogy |
|---|---|---|
| **15% Idle** | 85% of nodes generate revenue each month | Healthy, mature Pod with consistent pipeline |
| **30% Idle** | 70% active | Moderate pipeline. Some members between projects |
| **50% Idle** | Only half generating | Crisis mode. Economic downturn or startup phase |

At each month, every node independently has a probability of being "active" (generating revenue) or "idle" (producing zero). The Monte Carlo engine randomizes this across 100 runs and averages the results. This is not theoretical — it is the same stochastic modeling used in actuarial science.

## What We Learn — Scale by Scale

### 1. The Spark (2 Nodes, 36 Months)

Two founders at L2, operating at a minimalist 0.9c burn. This is the purest test of the system's genesis velocity.

**What the data tells us:** By month 36 at 85% active, the two founders accumulate **€245k in tax recapture** and **30.67c in Hub infrastructure** (≈€30,700). Their personal wealth averages 110c per node. The Safety pool of 18.40c provides a 6-month buffer for the pair.

**The critical question:** When should the Spark transition into a Pod? The data shows that by month 12–24, the Safety pool is large enough to onboard a third member through their ramp-up period without risking floor support. Waiting beyond month 24 is a missed opportunity — the infrastructure is begging for more builders.

**The risk:** At 30% idle, the treasury growth slows but never collapses. The Spark is resilient because two people can communicate perfectly. The limitation is not fragility — it is scale. Two founders cannot build the tier diversity needed for the Helix DNA royalties.

### 2. The Baseline Pod (5 Nodes, 60 Months)

Five builders at L2, no tier specialization. This is the team that says: "We're just starting. We don't have an Elder or an Architect yet."

**What the data tells us:** Even without tier diversity, five L2 Builders at 85% active accumulate **€1.0M in tax recapture** and **127c in Hub infrastructure** (≈€127k) over 60 months. The Safety buffer of 76c provides a **10-month runway** for all 5 nodes.

**The opportunity:** This configuration works. It is not optimal, but it is survivable and accumulative. The Baseline Pod is the stepping stone — it proves that you do not need a Perfect Circle to begin building sovereignty.

**The limitation:** Without a Leader / Founder-Elder (L4) or Architect (L3), the Pod lacks the mentorship gradient that unlocks the Apprentice pipeline and the high-value Helix DNA royalties. The weighted average productivity is 10c/node (pure L2), but could be 10.18c/node with tier diversity.

**Under stress (50% idle):** Even at half utilization, the Pod still accumulates 55c in Hub infrastructure and €432k in tax recapture. Zero floor failures. The system bends but does not break.

The [1:1:3:1:1] atomic strike team — 1 Leader, 1 Architect, 3 Builders, 1 Junior, 1 Apprentice. This is the minimum scale of true post-fragility.

**What the data tells us:** At 85% active, the Circle generates **71.25c/month** gross (billing €142,500/month to clients). By month 60:

**The floor-support mechanism in action:** The L0 Apprentice generates only 1.25c/month and keeps only 1.0c after the 80% split. Their 1.5c burn means they lose 0.5c/month. The Apprentice is not a cost; they are the system's future.

**The critical insight:** The 42% treasury reduction between 85% and 50% utilization is survivable. The system degrades gracefully, not catastrophically.

> **M = B / (1 - B)**
