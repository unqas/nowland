## 2. Income & Taxation (The Syntropy Split)

To an auditor, the 80/10/5/3/2 Split identifies how revenue is allocated within the holding array. 

### A. The 90% (Hub → Pod) — B2B Liability Cascade
The Hub does not owe liabilities directly to individual Nodes. The Hub logs **90%** of net revenue as a legal Liability (Accounts Payable) owed to the **Pod entity** and pays it as a 100% tax-deductible B2B expense. The Hub's taxable profit is instantly reduced to just its retained 5% + the 5% upstream (Grid/NET/Helix). The Hub manages only ~33 Pod vendors, not 230 individual humans.

### B. The 80% (Pod → Node) — Parent-Subsidiary Dividends
The Pod receives the 90% from the Hub and retains its 10% Treasury for shared pre-tax purchasing. The remaining **80%** can be distributed to the Node entity as a **Corporate Dividend** under the Parent-Subsidiary Directive (or local equivalent), typically 95–100% tax-free between companies. The human only triggers progressive PIT when pulling a salary from their own Micro-LLC.

### C. The 15% (Treasuries) — Statutory Reserves & Retained Earnings
Legally classified as **Statutory Cooperative Reserves** (Hub 5%) or **Retained Corporate Earnings** (Pod 10%). Rather than attempting to "expense" this to dodge taxation, the organism *proudly pays* CIT on this 15%. This instantly sanitizes the money, yielding clean, non-progressive corporate wealth to be invested in community physical assets.

### D. The 5% (Grid/NET/Helix) — Deductible Fees
Classified as **Software Licensing Fees** or **Intellectual Property Royalties**. Because the Grid and NET are nonprofits, these fees act as fully deductible corporate expenses for the Hub, neutralizing the final 5% before profit calculation.

## 3. Defensive Accounting Strategies

### Defeating "Constructive Receipt"
**The Argument:** Internal `c` units are not "cash equivalent." They represent unsettled B2B commercial obligations.
**The Proof:** The internal ledger functions as a **Mutual Credit Clearing House**. A Node Micro-LLC does not "receive money" personally when a `c` unit is logged. Commercial B2B revenue is only formally realized or accounted for when a milestone or fiscal cycle closes, dictating a consolidated B2B invoice be triggered between corporate entities.

### Defeating "Disguised Employment" (The Parent-Subsidiary Defense)
**The Argument:** The Hub operates exclusively via parent-subsidiary and B2B corporate logic, legally nullifying employee classification.
**The Proof:** 
1. **The Corporate Shield:** A B2B Cooperative does not hire a Micro-LLC "employee". By mandating every Node wrap themselves in an entity (S-Corp, UG, SASU), all transactions become pure corporate logistics across commercial supply chains.
2. **The Stigmergic Board:** By moving all work allocation to a non-profit-owned software board, the Hub has zero 'power of direction.' It acts exclusively as a passive financial clearing layer for supply/demand matches.

## 4. The Consilience Clause (Specific Performance)

In traditional contract law, you sue for "Damages." In the Nowland, we focus on **Specific Performance**. 
The CPL (Consilience Public License) is a **Source-Available License** that grants usage rights contingent on the performance of the Split. If a commercial entity copy-pastes the Nowland DNA but refuses to honor the 20% split, they are in material breach of license. 

Legal counsel should note that Article 5 of the CPL uses **Exile** as the primary remedy, but explicitly reserves the right to seek damages under standard contract law in high-value cases of "Parasitic Extraction."

## 5. Pod Equity Mechanics (The 16/12/8/4 Share Array)

The Pod (LLC, or equivalent) issues **Equity Shares** that mirror the level system. The 1:4 Max-Out Coefficient is structurally encoded in the corporate articles of association:

| Role | Legal Shares | Dividend Ratio |
|---|---|---|
| L4 — Leader | 16 | 4:1 |
| L3 — Architect | 12 | 3:1 |
| L2 — Builder | 8 | 2:1 |
| L1 — Junior | 4 | 1:1 |
| L0 — Apprentice | 0 (Phantom Shares) | Vesting |

**Phantom Shares:** Juniors and Apprentices receive their cash distribution via Profit Participation Points written into their B2B Contractor Agreement. They hold zero legal equity, zero voting rights, and zero claim on the Pod’s treasury. This is legally a standard B2B service arrangement.

**Vesting Trigger:** Once the Node’s micro-LLC generates 50c (~€125k) of verified kinetic energy, the Pod issues 8 legal equity shares. To avoid the "sweat equity" tax trap on issuance, the LLC issues *Profits Interests* (US) or the Node exercises a pre-agreed option to buy the shares at a nominal fiat fee (EU). The total share pool dilutes proportionally, but the 1:4 ratio is structurally preserved.

**Exit / Shotgun Clause:** All Pod operating agreements must include a mandatory buy-sell mechanism for deadlock resolution and member departure. Shares are bought back at nominal or depreciated book value.
