# Sharing the Harvest

*(or, How the Harvest is Split Among the Hands That Planted)*

> **TL;DR:** Within a Pod, the 80% Node allocation is further distributed according to a level system (L0–L4) anchored to the Pod's growth stage. Two co-founders split equally. As the Pod matures and secures its Baseline, higher contribution levels unlock — always governed by the 1:4 Max-Out Coefficient. You cannot have an Apex earner in a Pod where the Juniors are not yet fed.

---

Every chapter so far has described how the **Syntropy Split** fractures energy between the Node, the Pod, the Hub, the Helix, and the Grid. But what happens *inside* the Node allocation when a **team** executes a bounty together? How do you split the 80% among a founder, a senior architect, a mid-level builder, and a junior apprentice without recreating the extractive salary hierarchy of the fiat world?

The answer is **Sharing the Harvest** — a level-based system that activates progressively as the Pod matures.

---

## The Starting Position: Parity

When two co-founders create a Pod, there is no hierarchy. They are two cells dividing. The ratio is **1:1**. Both contribute, both share equally, both carry identical risk.

When a Pod grows beyond two co-founders and introduces members — contractors, narrow-field specialists, or junior builders — the founding Node who carries the overarching responsibility (finding clients, maintaining the Membrane, mentoring and motivating the team, making final calls when consensus fails) is recognized as the **Founder/Leader**. The base ratio between the Founder and other members is allowed to shift to **1:2** — the Founder can receive up to twice the base allocation of a standard member. This is not a privilege; it is a reward gained for the disproportionate risk, leadership responsibility, and mentorship burden the Leader carries for the Pod lifetime.

---

## The Level System (L0–L4)

Once a Pod generates surplus beyond its immediate survival needs, members can operate at different output levels. The level system governs how the harvest is shared. There are five **roles** (Apprentice, Junior, Builder, Architect, Leader) and five corresponding **distribution tiers** (MVB, Baseline, Mid, Senior, Apex). In general, roles match their distribution tier — but a Node who consistently performs above their role can earn at a higher tier by Pod consensus.

| Level | Role | Distribution Tier | Compensation Floor |
|---|---|---|---|
| **L0** | Apprentice (Intern / Youth 13+) | MVB | 1c–1.5c (floor, not ceiling) |
| **L1** | Junior | Baseline | 4c (biological peace floor) |
| **L2** | Builder | Mid | ~33% from Baseline to Apex (= **8c**) |
| **L3** | Architect | Senior | ~66% from Baseline to Apex (= **12c**) |
| **L4** | Leader / Founder-Elder | Apex | 100% of range (= **16c**) + treasury allocation responsibility |

**The math with current constants:**
- Baseline = 4c. Apex = 16c. The gap is 12c.
- L0 (Apprentice): **1c** (MVB floor, growing to ~1.5c once 1:3 ratio is active)
- L1 (Junior): **4c**
- L2 (Builder): 4c + (12c × 33.33%) = **8c**
- L3 (Architect): 4c + (12c × 66.67%) = **12c**
- L4 (Leader): 4c + (12c × 100%) = **16c**

**These are floors, not ceilings.** Every Node at every level can earn more than their floor. If the Pod is highly effective and consensus-raises its Baseline above 4c, all tiers scale proportionally. A Pod with a 25c consensus Baseline → L4 Apex = 100c.

The Leader at L4 additionally holds the right to make consensus-driven decisions on how **3% of the Pod's 10% treasury** is allocated or redistributed among the team — an executive stewardship responsibility, not a personal bonus.

**The Corporate Translation (The Share Array):**
When the Pod wraps itself in a corporate entity (LLC/LDA) under the Trojan Mouse strategy, these levels map directly onto the Pod’s **Equity Share Array**: L4 = 16 shares, L3 = 12, L2 = 8, L1 = 0 (Phantom), L0 = 0 (Phantom). Dividends are paid per share, structurally hardcoding the 1:4 Max-Out Coefficient into the corporate articles of association. Apprentices and Juniors (L0–L1) hold Phantom Shares (Profit Participation Points) that grant them their cash distribution ratio but zero legal equity or voting rights. They vest into real equity (8 Builder shares) once they cross the 50c energy threshold.

## The Progressive Growth Stages Unlocking Principle

These levels do not exist from day one. They activate progressively as the Pod matures through four distinct stages. This is the most important structural principle of the entire compensation system:

> **You cannot have an Apex earner in a Pod where the Juniors are not yet fed.**

The ceiling of the highest earner is mathematically locked to the floor of the lowest. This is not a metaphor — it is enforced by the growth stage triggers. If an ambitious Node wants to reach L4 compensation, they are biologically forced to first ensure that every other member of their Pod has cleared their Baseline. The organism cannot grow a canopy until the roots are fed.

| Stage | Trigger Condition | Max Ratio | Levels Available |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Genesis** | Pod is born. Everyone starts here. | 1:1 | L0–L1 only — all energy directed at securing the Pod's consensus MVB |
| **MVB Reached** | Every member clears the consensus MVB sustainably | 1:2 | L2 unlocked — founding risk is recognized |
| **Baseline Reached** | Every member clears 4c/month sustained | 1:3 | L3 unlocked — senior contributions are rewarded |
| **Reserve Secured** | Pod stores `4c × members × 6` months in its treasury | 1:4 | L4 unlocked — full Apex range available, 1:4 Max-Out active |

### The Reserve Threshold

The final stage — unlocking the full 1:4 ratio — requires the Pod to have stored six months of Baseline for every member. This is the mathematical proof that the Pod is not just surviving, but *thriving*.

Not every Pod is a Perfect Circle of seven. The reserve threshold scales to whatever your Pod actually is:
- A Pod of **5 members** must store `4c × 5 × 6 = **120c**` before any member can operate at L4.
- A Pod of **7 members** (the Perfect Circle) must store `4c × 7 × 6 = **168c**` before L4 unlocks.

The Perfect Circle requires time to reach. Many Pods will operate as 3–5 member teams for months or years before growing to seven. That is completely normal, and that is completely fine. The math works at every size.

Why six months? Because six months of reserve means the entire Pod can survive a complete economic winter without generating a single unit of new Syntropy. This is the biological equivalent of a tree storing enough sugar in its roots to survive winter. If your roots are full, your canopy can grow tall.


## Weight Factors for Redistribution

Within each level, the precise allocation is not a flat number — it is weighted by three factors:

1. **LSS (Lifetime Syntropy Score):** Your proven track record. A Node with an LSS of 500 has demonstrated more cumulative value creation than a Node with an LSS of 50. This is not seniority by time — it is seniority by output. The LSS itself is split 80/20 between the Executor and the Vacuum-creator (see *Appendix: LSS Attribution*).
2. **Throughput:** How much energy has flowed through you in the *current* Hub/Pod. A high-LSS Node who recently joined a new Pod has historical credibility but limited local contribution. Throughput weights the recent, lived reality.
3. **Join Date:** How early you committed to the organism. The first Nodes in a Pod carried the highest risk — they joined before the Baseline was secured, before the treasury existed, before the proof was in. Skin in the game is recognized.

These three factors are multiplied together to produce a **Redistribution Weight** that determines the precise position within the level's range. The Pod's consensus governs how heavily each factor is weighted — some Pods may value LSS above all else, others may prioritize local throughput.

### How LSS Attribution and Bounty Distribution Interact

These two systems operate on different axes:

*   **LSS Attribution** (see *Appendix: LSS Attribution*) governs **who gets credit** — the signal. When a Vacuum is filled, the Executor earns 80% of the LSS points and the Vacuum-creator earns 20%. This determines your reputation and governance weight.
*   **Bounty Distribution** governs **who gets paid** — the harvest. The level system (L0–L4) determines your compensation tier based on your role and the Pod's maturity stage.

Both feed into the Redistribution Weight. Your LSS (earned through credited circuits) determines your seniority score, which — combined with Throughput and Join Date — determines your precise position within your level's compensation range. The scout who identifies valuable Vacuums accumulates LSS credit through the 20% attribution, which over time elevates their Redistribution Weight even if their kinetic role is Builder-level.

## Before Sustainability: The Attribution Ledger

A critical design principle: **the work you do before the Pod reaches sustainability is not free labor.** 

When you are in the Genesis stage, fighting to secure MVB, you write down everything you create. You track every hour, every deliverable, every unit of energy you generate — even when the Pod cannot yet afford to pay you for it. This creates an **Attribution Ledger** — a historical record of who built the foundation.

Once the Pod reaches sustainability (10c revenue per person), the surplus has a history. The Attribution Ledger ensures that the founders who built the engine during the cold months receive retroactive recognition — either as a one-time redistribution from the surplus, or as a permanently higher Redistribution Weight that reflects their founding contribution.

You are not working for free. You are investing in an organism that remembers.

**The Legal Reality:** During the Genesis stage, Nodes who have not yet vested into the Pod’s corporate entity operate as external B2B contractors holding Phantom Shares. The Attribution Ledger serves as the mathematical proof that their early sweat-equity justifies the share issuance when the vesting threshold (50c) is crossed. This keeps the corporate cap table clean while preserving the historical fairness of early contribution.

> ### The Core Principle
> **The harvest is shared by the hands that planted the seeds, in proportion to the weight of the seeds they carried.** A Pod does not have salaries. It has levels. And the levels do not unlock by title or negotiation — they unlock by the Pod's collective success. You cannot reach the Apex unless you have pulled every member of your Pod to the Baseline first. The forest grows tallest where the roots are deepest.
