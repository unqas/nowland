## The Optimized Work Week

Before we calculate anything, we must define the biological schedule. The fiat world forces a 5-day, 8-hour, 40-hour workweek — a number pulled from nineteenth-century factory economics, not from human biology. It is not accounting for human needs, nor for the technological acceleration of the current high-concurrency pace of intensive AI-assisted work.

In The Nowland, the optimized biological schedule is **4 days a week, 7 hours a day**. That equals **28 hours a week**, or exactly **112 hours a month**.

This is not laziness. This is physics. Human creative output is not linear. After 6–7 hours of deep kinetic work, the quality of output degrades exponentially. The remaining hours a fiat employee spends at their desk are pure administrative friction — emails about emails, meetings about meetings — generating heat but no light.

By working 4 days and resting 3, the builder maintains a consistently high output per hour, avoids the chronic burnout that destroys long-term productivity, and reserves energy for the non-kinetic activities that sustain the organism: cooking, mentoring, resting, exploring.

### The MVB — A Triple Function

The MVB (Minimal Viable Baseline) serves three distinct mathematical functions in the system:

1. **Pod Survival Floor:** The MVB is the defined minimal viable baseline for a team that is starting out, or for a Hub that is going through a major crisis and doesn't have enough resources to cover the full 4c Baseline for everyone. It is the "everyone survives" number.
2. **Child Support Unit:** 1 child costs approximately **1c** per month. When there are 2 children, they share economies of scale: **1.25c** (0.625c each). When there are 3 children: **1.5c** (0.5c each). This unit scales with the family structure, not linearly per head.
3. **Apprentice Floor:** The MVB is the baseline compensation for the Apprentice tier — whether that Apprentice is a youth of 13+ learning to earn their own child support (unloading the parent), or a newcomer joining the Pod on a test period. The Apprentice starts at MVB and grows from there.

## The 1:4 Max-Out Coefficient 

If a Node can generate 50c of value, what stops them from turning into an exact replica of a fiat CEO, earning millions while the other Nodes in their Pod languish at the minimum Baseline?

The system mathematically prohibits it through the **Max-Out Coefficient**. 

By default, the internal ledger strictly enforces a **1:4 biological wealth ratio** inside a Pod. This means the highest earner in a Pod cannot exceed a balance that is more than four times higher than the lowest earner's Baseline.

The ratio derives directly from the Generation Stack:
- `4c` = Baseline survival (the biological floor).
- `16c` = Full biological peace at the default Baseline (the Apex ceiling).
- `16c ÷ 4c = 4:1` — the default ratio, because the system optimizes for *uplift*, not for maximum individual extraction.

**Example:** If you want to achieve 16c of wealth, you absolutely cannot do it unless *every single other person in your Pod* has achieved at least 4c. 

But the Baseline itself is not fixed at 4c forever. As your Pod grows, as your products find their market, and as your collective efficiency increases, the Pod can **consensus-raise** its internal Baseline. If your team is highly effective — bringing massive revenue with high-quality products — and everyone agrees to raise the Baseline to 25c, then the Apex scales to 100c. The 1:4 ratio holds; it is the *floor* that rises, carrying the entire ceiling with it. Raising the Baseline is a consensus-driven decision: it means the Pod collectively agrees that their new biological peace standard is higher than the minimum.

You *must* raise the bar for everyone. You must mentor them, share your high-value Vacuums with them, route bounties their way, and optimize their workflows. Your personal wealth ceiling is bolted directly to the floor of your weakest member. 

This is not altruism. This is thermodynamics. The organ cannot grow if its cells are dying. The builder cannot soar if their strike-team is starving. The 1:4 Coefficient forces every ambitious Node to become a leader who lifts, not an executive who extracts.
